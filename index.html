Various educators teach rules governing the length of paragraphs. They may say that a paragraph should be 100 to 200 words long, or be no more than five or six sentences.

This example demonstrates the getElementsByTagName method.

A paragraph is a series of related sentences developing a central idea, called the topic.

Try to think about paragraphs in terms of thematic unity: a paragraph is a sentence or a group of sentences that supports one central, unified idea. Paragraphs add one idea at a time to your broader argument.

But a good paragraph should not be measured in characters, words, or sentences. The true measure of your paragraphs should be ideas.
